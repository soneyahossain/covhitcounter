89    /**
90    * Returns a word based formatter for the specified locale.
91    * <p>
92    * The words are configured in a resource bundle text file -
93    * {@code org.joda.time.format.messages}.
94    * This can be added to via the normal classpath resource bundle mechanisms.
95    * <p>
96    * You can add your own translation by creating messages_<locale>.properties file
97    * and adding it to the {@code org.joda.time.format.messages} path.
98    * <p>
99    * Simple example (1 -> singular suffix, not 1 -> plural suffix):
100    *
101    * <pre>
102    * PeriodFormat.space=\
103    * PeriodFormat.comma=,
104    * PeriodFormat.commandand=,and
105    * PeriodFormat.commaspaceand=, and
106    * PeriodFormat.commaspace=,
107    * PeriodFormat.spaceandspace=\ and
108    * PeriodFormat.year=\ year
109    * PeriodFormat.years=\ years
110    * PeriodFormat.month=\ month
111    * PeriodFormat.months=\ months
112    * PeriodFormat.week=\ week
113    * PeriodFormat.weeks=\ weeks
114    * PeriodFormat.day=\ day
115    * PeriodFormat.days=\ days
116    * PeriodFormat.hour=\ hour
117    * PeriodFormat.hours=\ hours
118    * PeriodFormat.minute=\ minute
119    * PeriodFormat.minutes=\ minutes
120    * PeriodFormat.second=\ second
121    * PeriodFormat.seconds=\ seconds
122    * PeriodFormat.millisecond=\ millisecond
123    * PeriodFormat.milliseconds=\ milliseconds
124    * </pre>
125    *
126    * <p>
127    * Some languages contain more than two suffixes. You can use regular expressions
128    * for them. Here's an example using regular expression for English:
129    *
130    * <pre>
131    * PeriodFormat.space=\
132    * PeriodFormat.comma=,
133    * PeriodFormat.commandand=,and
134    * PeriodFormat.commaspaceand=, and
135    * PeriodFormat.commaspace=,
136    * PeriodFormat.spaceandspace=\ and
137    * PeriodFormat.regex.separator=%
138    * PeriodFormat.years.regex=1$%.*
139    * PeriodFormat.years.list=\ year%\ years
140    * PeriodFormat.months.regex=1$%.*
141    * PeriodFormat.months.list=\ month%\ months
142    * PeriodFormat.weeks.regex=1$%.*
143    * PeriodFormat.weeks.list=\ week%\ weeks
144    * PeriodFormat.days.regex=1$%.*
145    * PeriodFormat.days.list=\ day%\ days
146    * PeriodFormat.hours.regex=1$%.*
147    * PeriodFormat.hours.list=\ hour%\ hours
148    * PeriodFormat.minutes.regex=1$%.*
149    * PeriodFormat.minutes.list=\ minute%\ minutes
150    * PeriodFormat.seconds.regex=1$%.*
151    * PeriodFormat.seconds.list=\ second%\ seconds
152    * PeriodFormat.milliseconds.regex=1$%.*
153    * PeriodFormat.milliseconds.list=\ millisecond%\ milliseconds
154    * </pre>
155    *
156    * <p>
157    * You can mix both approaches. Here's example for Polish (
158    * "1 year, 2 years, 5 years, 12 years, 15 years, 21 years, 22 years, 25 years"
159    * translates to
160    * "1 rok, 2 lata, 5 lat, 12 lat, 15 lat, 21 lat, 22 lata, 25 lat"). Notice that
161    * PeriodFormat.day and PeriodFormat.days is used for day suffixes as there is no
162    * need for regular expressions:
163    *
164    * <pre>
165    * PeriodFormat.space=\
166    * PeriodFormat.comma=,
167    * PeriodFormat.commandand=,i
168    * PeriodFormat.commaspaceand=, i
169    * PeriodFormat.commaspace=,
170    * PeriodFormat.spaceandspace=\ i
171    * PeriodFormat.regex.separator=%
172    * PeriodFormat.years.regex=^1$%[0-9]*(?&lt;!1)[2-4]$%[0-9]*
173    * PeriodFormat.years.list=\ rok%\ lata%\ lat
174    * PeriodFormat.months.regex=^1$%[0-9]*(?&lt;!1)[2-4]$%[0-9]*
175    * PeriodFormat.months.list=\ miesi\u0105c%\ miesi\u0105ce%\ miesi\u0119cy
176    * PeriodFormat.weeks.regex=^1$%[0-9]*(?&lt;!1)[2-4]$%[0-9]*
177    * PeriodFormat.weeks.list=\ tydzie\u0144%\ tygodnie%\ tygodni
178    * PeriodFormat.day=\ dzie\u0144
179    * PeriodFormat.days=\ dni
180    * PeriodFormat.hours.regex=^1$%[0-9]*(?&lt;!1)[2-4]$%[0-9]*
181    * PeriodFormat.hours.list=\ godzina%\ godziny%\ godzin
