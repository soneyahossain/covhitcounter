27    /**
28    * Implements the Coptic calendar system, which defines every fourth year as
29    * leap, much like the Julian calendar. The year is broken down into 12 months,
30    * each 30 days in length. An extra period at the end of the year is either 5
31    * or 6 days in length. In this implementation, it is considered a 13th month.
32    * <p>
33    * Year 1 in the Coptic calendar began on August 29, 284 CE (Julian), thus
34    * Coptic years do not begin at the same time as Julian years. This chronology
35    * is not proleptic, as it does not allow dates before the first Coptic year.
36    * <p>
37    * This implementation defines a day as midnight to midnight exactly as per
38    * the ISO chronology. Some references indicate that a coptic day starts at
39    * sunset on the previous ISO day, but this has not been confirmed and is not
40    * implemented.
41    * <p>
42    * CopticChronology is thread-safe and immutable.
43    *
44    * @author Brian S O'Neill
45    * @see <a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Coptic_calendar">Wikipedia</a>

48    */
